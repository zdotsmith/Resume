Dissertation Project

Woody Debris Collision Forces on Bridge Piers

Conducted comprehensive research investigating forces exerted by woody debris collisions on bridge piers during flooding events. Utilized SolidWorks for 3D modeling and printing of scaled bridge pier models. Implemented sophisticated data collection using load cells and applied statistical analysis through MATLAB and Python. Key findings revealed linear relationships between flow velocity and peak forces, with quadratic trends in drag force measurements.

Skills demonstrated: Research methodology, experimental design, data analysis, 3D modeling, statistical analysis, technical writing.
